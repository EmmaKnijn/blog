# AI and the quality of the internet
We all know that AI has taken the internet by storm since 2023, especially generative AI in the form of LLMs. While there might be *some* amount of use in an LLM (for example for translation or parsing esoteric data formats), image and video generation is ***completely*** useless and only serves to disrupt or destroy the economy (more than LLMs already do). This along with the unethical sourcing of the training data and the environmental impact of generative AI makes me really reconsider the use of it, however, I think that is only part of the issue with AI.

## Training
LLMs have been trained on petabytes upon petabytes of textual content. During this millions of physical books have been destroyed(1), at least 82TB of books have been pirated(2) and the entire internet has been scraped multiple times over for training data. Just this alone has put a significant burden on the environment.

## AI inbreeding
Because of the unquenchable thirst by AI companies to get more data for LLM training, AI companies have continued to scrape the internet. This happens even after (primarily) ChatGPT has been abused to create a quick buck for a couple people because they keep creating fake pages and websites. The quality of these pages is very poor, to say the least.
New LLMs have been trained on this bad quality text, which causes regressions in its training and way worse output over time. I believe that LLM improvements will very quickly hit a brick wall, partly because of this.

## The degradation of search
Because all of this SEO-optimised slop has been pushed to the internet, the quality of search results already started degrading in 2024.
